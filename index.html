Welcome to McCarty's Game Programming Workshop!

Thanks for coming! Stick around for the next two hours and learn how to program a game. It's actually not that hard at all. If you're ever lost or need help, ask John, Caleb, or Clara for help.

The first thing you need to do is get into groups. We recommend teams of 2-4, but if you want to go solo, or have a bigger party, we won't stop you.

The next step is to figure out what level your at. There are two tracks you can follow: beginner or advanced. Their outlined below, and it's important that you choose the one that best represents your skill level, otherwise you'll be bored or lost. If choose one and decide later that you don't like it, don't be afraid to switch to the other!

Beginner

This uses Scratch, a visual programming language designed designed by MIT. Everything is done completely through the web browser, and nothing has to be downloaded. It comes with an easy to use interface, that beginners will love.

Recommended if:

  • Have not taken a programming course
  • Are scared of 3D programs
  • Really, really, really, hate python

Advanced

This uses Python and OpenGL. It provides you with a working (basic) clone of minecraft, and points you in directions you can take to extend and modify it. You could play with OpenGL and try to modify the graphics, or could extend the logic of the game.

Recommended if:

  • Have taken CSE 143 or higher level programming courses
  • Want to do something challenging
  • Aren't afraid to explore and do things independently
  • Sick of Java and want to try a similar easier language out
